Form A11y

Duration: July 2021 - Present

Engagement Type: Collaboration with Adobe Research.

Team Size: 1

Role: I’ve been working on both design and development for the project. Though, through the project, I have been guided by Prof. Jonathan Lazar and the amazing team at Adobe Research.

Objective

Design a tool that makes the process of making PDF Forms accessible easier for users.

Defining the problem

We had empirical evidences that existing tools used to make PDF Forms accessible are hard to use. However, we did not know the exact problems.To learn more about the problem domain, I explored existing tools and already accessible forms (such as IRS forms).

Further, I interviewed two people who made PDFs accessible for a living with the goal of – 1) learning about their processes, 2) Existing tools and their capabilities, 3) Issues they’ve had with the tools in the past.

Based on the investigation, I was able to identify the exact problem people faced. Even though existing tools had all features needed to make PDF Forms accessible – 1) the process to making forms accessible was non-intuitive, 2) fixing forms with a large number of fields can get really tedious.

To understand gravity of the issue, imagine making each field of this PDF form accessible. When, making each field accessible takes a minimum of 3 steps. Btw, this just the first page of the 4-page form.

Ideations

Even though, a user interface could have been designed that would make the process intuitive. Users would still have go through the tedious process of fixing each element on the form. This clearly required a technical solution. So, I contacted the research team at Adobe with my findings and they directed me to some research in the area of document intelligence machine learning models.

Based on my exploration, I found that current state-of-the-art ML models can detect fields, text and hierarchies in a form. This provided me with more capabilities in terms of technologies that I can leverage in the user interface. However, machine learning models come with their own problems. Biggest problem being that they almost never are 100% accurate i.e. suggestions from models have errors.

Thus, human intervention is required to review the work done by models and fix all the mistakes the model made. Essentially, this tool is an interface between users and machine learning models.

I started brainstorming solutions and creating designs for the tool. Each week, I had discussion with the team to discuss the shortcomings and newer ideas for the design. Further, I performed basic usability test on the low-fi designs. Based on the feedback, I reiterated over the designs.

Helping users review changes made by the model

Fixing errors introduced by the model.

Bulk operations on elements in the PDF Form.

Fixing for high density of information on interface.

Prototyping

Currently, I am developing a high-fidelity prototype of the tool in ReactJS. The prototype will be used for usability testing and report the findings in the form of a research paper.
